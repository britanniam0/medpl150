Reflection on Nuclear War Prevention

The New York Times' interactive on nuclear war prevention is a fairly long piece, but it was surprisingly easy to get through because of its use of illustrations and visual explanations. Even though the article covers a heavy, complex topic, the visuals made it far more engaging and understandable. Instead of feeling overwhelmed by text, I found myself pulled deeper into the content because of the way the illustrations guided me through the information. The visual storytelling helped put the scale of nuclear destruction into perspective in a way plain words alone never could.

One thing that stood out to me is how the creators chose illustrations rather than real photographs. This choice was incredibly effective. Photographs of nuclear devastation would be too graphic, too overwhelming, and possibly even numbing because of their brutality.
